# Triage Rules — How to sort each email

Use these definitions to put every message in exactly one bucket. If a message
could fit two, choose the one **higher up this list** (higher attention wins).

---

## 🔴 Triage/Urgent
The message needs the user's attention **today**, and delay causes a real
problem. Signals:
- A person (not an automated system) is directly asking the user for something
  time-sensitive.
- Words like "urgent", "ASAP", "today", "EOD", "deadline", "final notice".
- A meeting, call, or deliverable within the next 24 hours.
- A reply from someone the user is clearly waiting on for a decision.
- Anything involving money owed, legal, security, or an account being locked.

**Reply usually needed: yes.**

---

## 🟠 Triage/Action-Needed
The user has to *do* something, but it is not on fire. Signals:
- A request, question, or task addressed to the user, due in the coming days.
- A form to fill, a document to review, an approval to give.
- A scheduling request ("can you meet next week?").

**Reply usually needed: yes.**

---

## 🟡 Triage/Waiting-On
The ball is in someone else's court; the user is waiting for them. Signals:
- The user previously asked a question and this is *not yet* the answer (e.g. an
  auto-acknowledgement, "we'll get back to you").
- A status update where no action is requested of the user.

**Reply usually needed: no** (unless it is a nudge the user would want to send).

---

## 🟡 Triage/FYI
Informational. The user should be aware but nothing is required. Signals:
- CC'd for visibility, not addressed directly.
- Announcements, internal updates, "no action needed" notes.

**Reply usually needed: no.**

---

## 🟢 Triage/Newsletter
Bulk / marketing / subscription content. Signals:
- "Unsubscribe" link, from a `no-reply@` or marketing address.
- Digests, promotions, product updates, blogs.

**Reply usually needed: no.** Just label and count.

---

## 🟢 Triage/Receipts
Transaction records the user may want to keep. Signals:
- Order confirmations, invoices, payment receipts, shipping notices, tickets.

**Reply usually needed: no.** Label so they are easy to find later.

---

## ⚠️ Triage/Spam-Suspicious
Looks unsafe or deceptive — **flag only, never act destructively.** Signals:
- Unexpected attachments or links asking for login/payment info (phishing).
- Sender name and email address do not match, or the domain looks off.
- Pressure + a link + a request for credentials or money.
- "You've won", "verify your account", "your package is held" from odd senders.

**Reply usually needed: no.** Apply the label and tell the user in the briefing
to look at these carefully. Do **not** click links, do **not** reply, do **not**
delete — leave the final call to the user.

---

## Tie-breakers & judgment
- A real human asking directly > an automated system.
- A deadline this week > no deadline.
- When unsure between Action-Needed and FYI → choose **Action-Needed**.
- When unsure whether something is phishing → choose **Spam-Suspicious** and
  say why in the briefing.
- Known senders (the user's colleagues, family, clients) should rarely land in
  Newsletter/Spam — double-check before down-ranking a real person.
