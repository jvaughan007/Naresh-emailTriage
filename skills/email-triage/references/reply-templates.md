# Reply Templates — Tone and patterns for drafts

These are **starting points**, not scripts. Adapt to the specific message, match
the sender's formality, and keep replies short. Every draft stays in the Drafts
folder for the user to review — nothing is ever sent automatically.

## Golden rules for every draft
- **Never invent facts.** If you need a date, price, or decision you don't have,
  insert a visible placeholder: `[[Naresh: confirm the meeting time]]`.
- Mirror the sender's greeting/sign-off style (formal vs. casual).
- One clear purpose per reply. Don't over-explain.
- Sign as the user. If a signature block is unknown, use a simple `Best,\nNaresh`
  and leave a placeholder if a title/company is needed.

---

## 1. Acknowledge + will follow up
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for this — I've received it and will get back to you by
> [[Naresh: date]]. Appreciate your patience.
>
> Best,
> Naresh

## 2. Answer a simple question (only if the answer is truly known)
> Hi [Name],
>
> Good question — [[Naresh: the answer]]. Let me know if you need anything else.
>
> Best,
> Naresh

## 3. Scheduling — propose or confirm a time
> Hi [Name],
>
> Happy to meet. I'm free [[Naresh: option 1]] or [[Naresh: option 2]] —
> whichever works best for you. I'll send a calendar invite once we lock it in.
>
> Best,
> Naresh

## 4. Decline / not now, politely
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for thinking of me. I won't be able to [[Naresh: the ask]] right now,
> but [[Naresh: alternative or "let's revisit later"]]. Appreciate your
> understanding.
>
> Best,
> Naresh

## 5. Ask for missing information
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for reaching out. To move this forward, could you share
> [[Naresh: the specific info needed]]? Once I have that I'll [[Naresh: next step]].
>
> Best,
> Naresh

## 6. Quick internal / casual reply
> Hi [Name] — got it, thanks. [[Naresh: any short follow-up]]. Will do.
>
> Naresh

---

## When NOT to draft a reply
- Newsletters, receipts, and pure-FYI messages → no draft.
- Suspicious/phishing messages → **never reply.** Flag only.
- Anything where you would have to guess at a decision only the user can make →
  draft the shell with placeholders, but make the placeholders obvious so the
  user knows it needs their input before sending.
